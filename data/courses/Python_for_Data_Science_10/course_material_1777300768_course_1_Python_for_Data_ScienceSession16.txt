Session 16: Final Capstone Presentation

Welcome to the final session of Python for Data Science! Today is the culmination of eight weeks of hard work. You have transitioned from writing basic Python scripts to building predictive machine learning models.

This session is dedicated to your Capstone Presentations, where you will showcase your end-to-end data product to the class.

1. Presentation Structure (10 Minutes)

Your presentation should tell a story. Move beyond the code and focus on the why and the value of your findings.

The Problem Statement (1 min): What question are you trying to answer? Why does this matter to a business or organization?

Data Acquisition (1 min): Where did your data come from? (API, Web Scraping, or Public Dataset).

Data Wrangling & Cleaning (2 mins): What were the biggest challenges with the raw data? How did you handle nulls and outliers?

Exploratory Data Analysis (3 mins): Show your most impactful visualizations. What correlations or trends did you find?

Modeling & Results (2 mins): Which algorithm did you choose (Regression, Classification, or Clustering)? How accurate was it?

Conclusion & Insights (1 min): What is the "actionable insight"? What should a stakeholder do based on your report?

2. Technical Requirements

To pass the capstone, your project must demonstrate proficiency in the following:

Pandas/NumPy: Efficient data manipulation without unnecessary loops.

Visualization: At least three distinct plot types (e.g., a Heatmap, a Boxplot, and a Time-Series line plot).

Machine Learning: A trained Scikit-Learn model with performance metrics (MSE, $R^2$, or Accuracy).

Code Quality: Clean, commented code following PEP 8 standards.
